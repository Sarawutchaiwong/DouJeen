## Design Context

### Users
DouJeen serves Gen Z Chinese learners who find traditional memorization boring, plus puzzle-game fans and curious explorers. They use the game across phones, tablets, and desktops to combine characters, discover new concepts, hear pronunciations, and grow a personal collection without exams, timers, or competitive pressure.

### Brand Personality
Playful, encouraging, and curious. The product is a “Kawaii Alchemist” experience: a cozy, high-fidelity discovery laboratory where each successful combination creates a small, satisfying “Aha!” moment. The voice stays concise, warm, and clean rather than childish or corporate.

### Aesthetic Direction
Use the established “Bubbly Exploration” visual language: a light cream laboratory canvas, pastel mint and peach discovery cues, vibrant sky-blue actions, bold readable Chinese characters, organic rounded forms, generous breathing room, and soft tactile depth. Avoid Anki-like grids, competitive game UI, corporate gray, dark neon aesthetics, and decorative complexity that competes with the combine-and-discover loop.

### Design Principles
1. Preserve interaction parity by input capability, not viewport width: coarse-pointer and non-hover devices must always receive visible tap controls, including tablets.
2. Make every character feel immediately playable with clear 44px-or-larger touch targets, visible focus treatment, and responsive pressed feedback.
3. Keep the core loop legible at a glance: combine first, celebrate discovery second, collect third.
4. Use motion and sound to communicate state changes, while keeping feedback brief, compositor-friendly, and compatible with reduced-motion preferences.
5. Favor calm, accessible clarity over density: retain the pastel laboratory palette, strong contrast, readable Chinese typography, and generous spacing across orientations.
